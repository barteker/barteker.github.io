W/AV

Trailer for W/AV ("wave"), a streaming hardware device designed to bring the feeling and control of analog audio back into the digital age. More at wavmusicplayer.com

The Problem

Streaming made music infinite, and forgettable.

Algorithmic feeds, on-screen everything, and an endless library have flattened how we listen. Music has become background fuel — something to scroll past on the way to the next thing.

W/AV is a response to that: a dedicated piece of hardware built around the act of listening itself, instead of around another glowing screen.

The Product

A music player you can feel.

W/AV is a small, handheld streaming device with weighted knobs, physical buttons, and a small waveform display in place of the touchscreen most music devices default to. The interface is intentionally limited — no apps to bounce between, no notifications, no algorithmic suggestions. Just the song, and the controls to shape it.

  • Tactile controls — knobs and buttons sized for muscle memory, not menus.
  • Waveform display — a low-key visual readout of what's playing, instead of a full UI.
  • No touchscreen — listening shouldn't compete with everything else a screen wants from you.
